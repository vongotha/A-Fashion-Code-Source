Why Does My Size Differ Between Brands?

It is a common experience for individuals to find that the clothing size they typically wear varies depending on the brand. This discrepancy arises because the fashion industry lacks a universal standard for sizing.

Each brand often establishes its own set of 'standard' sizes, influenced by a multitude of factors. These factors can include the brand's target demographic and the prevailing sizing norms in their primary markets.

For instance, a brand catering to a North American audience might adhere to different sizing conventions than a brand primarily selling in Europe or Asia.

My Measurements Fall Between Sizes.

It is not uncommon for individuals to find that their body measurements fall between two sizes on a clothing size chart. In such instances, several factors should be considered to help you make the most appropriate choice. As a general guideline, particularly for unisex sizing, it is often recommended to opt for the larger size. This is because it is typically easier to have a garment that is slightly too large tailored to fit than it is to alter one that is too small.

The style of the garment should also influence your decision. For items that are designed to be fitted, such as bodycon dresses or tailored shirts, sizing up might provide a more comfortable and flattering fit, especially if your measurements are closer to the larger size. Conversely, for looser or more relaxed styles, such as oversized sweaters or flowy tops, the smaller size might be preferable to avoid the garment appearing too voluminous. Your personal preference regarding how you like your clothes to fit – whether you prefer a snug or a more relaxed feel – should also be taken into account.

If your hip measurement is significantly larger than your bust and waist measurements, and the garment in question has a flared skirt, it is often advisable to order based on your bust size, as the fuller skirt will likely accommodate the larger hips. For those who frequently fluctuate between sizes, investing in stretch clothing can be a practical solution, as these garments can often adapt to slight changes in body measurements. Elasticated waistbands can also offer greater flexibility in fit. In some cases, particularly with wrap dresses, the design itself can be quite forgiving in terms of sizing.

How Do I Measure Myself Accurately?

Achieving the best fit when shopping for clothes online hinges on the accuracy of your body measurements. To ensure you select the right size, it is essential to take precise measurements of key areas of your body. The following steps provide a comprehensive guide:

  1. Gather Your Tools: You will need a flexible measuring tape, preferably one with both inches and centimeters. A mirror can also be helpful to ensure the tape is positioned correctly. For certain measurements, having a friend assist you can improve accuracy.
  2. Prepare for Measurement: Wear lightweight, form-fitting clothing or just your undergarments to avoid adding bulk that could distort your measurements. Stand straight with good posture in front of a mirror if you are measuring yourself.
  3. Key Measurements:
    • Bust/Chest: For the bust, measure around the fullest part of your chest, under your arms, and across your shoulder blades. Ensure the tape is level and snug but not too tight.1 For men, measure around the broadest part of the chest, under the armpits and across the back.
    • Waist: Find your natural waistline, which is typically the narrowest part of your torso, often located around your belly button or slightly above. Wrap the measuring tape around your waist, keeping it parallel to the floor. Do not suck in your stomach.
    • Inseam: This measurement is for pants. Measure from the crotch seam down the inside of your leg to where you want the hem to fall, typically around your ankle bone. This is often easier with assistance.
    • Shoulder Width: Measure across your back from the tip of one shoulder bone to the tip of the other.
    • Sleeve Length: With your arm slightly bent, measure from the tip of your shoulder down to your wrist.
  4. Record Your Measurements: Write down your measurements in both inches and centimeters for future reference.
  5. Compare to Size Charts: When shopping online, always compare your measurements to the specific size chart provided for each item you are interested in.

By diligently following these steps, you can obtain accurate body measurements that will significantly enhance your chances of selecting clothing that fits well when purchasing online. Keeping these measurements readily available will streamline your online shopping experience.

What's the Difference Between Sizes?

Navigating the world of international clothing sizes can be perplexing, as different regions utilize distinct sizing systems. The three most common systems encountered are those used in the United States (US), the United Kingdom (UK), and Europe (EU). It is important to recognize that a specific size in one system may not directly correspond to the same size in another.

For example, a woman's size Medium in the US is not necessarily equivalent to a size Medium in Europe. Furthermore, sizing can vary even within the same size designation across these different systems. Notably, sizing conventions can also differ significantly in other regions; for instance, Japanese sizes generally tend to run smaller compared to their Western counterparts.

Given these discrepancies, it is crucial to avoid relying solely on general size conversion charts and instead prioritize consulting the specific size chart provided by the brand from which you are purchasing. Direct conversions between US and UK sizes are not always straightforward, and it is always advisable to check your body measurements against the brand's UK size guide.

While EU sizes can often be converted relatively easily to UK sizes, it remains essential to verify your bust and waist measurements against the brand's chart to ensure an accurate fit. Some brands may also have their own unique sizing variations that deviate from standard conversions. Therefore, the most reliable method for determining the correct size when shopping internationally is to take your body measurements and compare them to the brand's specific size chart, paying close attention to the measurement units provided (inches or centimeters).
